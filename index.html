Shakuntala Devi

the Human Computer

Shakuntala Devi was an Indian mathematician, writer and mental calculator, popularly known as the "Human Computer". Her talent earned her a place in the 1982 edition of The Guinness Book of World Records. Devi was a precocious child and she demonstrated her arithmetic abilities at the University of Mysore without any formal education.

About

Shakuntala Devi was born on 4th November 1929, to C V Sundararaja Rao, in Bangalore Karnataka. At age of 3, with no formal education, she showed first signs of Computing Arithematic problems without pen or paper. Her Father used to take her on Road shows, to demonstrate her talent to th world. At age of 6, she demonstrated Arithematic abitilty at University of Mysore. In 1944, Devi moved to London, UK. In addition to her 'mental calculator', Devi was a notable astrologer, authored several books, cookbooks and novels. Know more
